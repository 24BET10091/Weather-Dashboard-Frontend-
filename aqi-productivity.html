AQI and Productivity

Air pollution affects focus, reaction time, and comfort. Studies link elevated PM2.5 and poor AQI with reduced concentration, lower work performance, and greater fatigue, especially during prolonged indoor exposure.

Why productivity drops

  • High PM2.5 and ozone can trigger irritation that distracts users and lowers comfort.
  • Poor air quality can increase fatigue, headaches, and difficulty concentrating.
  • Schools and offices often see lower performance when AQI worsens during the day.

What to do when AQI is poor

  • Move work or exercise indoors where filtration is available.
  • Schedule demanding tasks for periods with better air quality.
  • Use air purifiers and ventilate carefully when pollution is low outdoors.

Why this dashboard helps

Live weather and AQI updates help users adjust plans in real time. People on laptops and phones can instantly see whether conditions are suitable for outdoor work, exercise, or study.

Suggested habits

  • Check AQI before commuting or outdoor meetings.
  • Prefer indoor tasks when the AQI reaches unhealthy levels.
  • Use the live weather overlay to understand whether rain or storms are affecting travel.
